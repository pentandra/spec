Introduction

Research is everywhere. From informal, such as ad hoc journalism investigations, to consumer reports, to formal research, such as stem cell research, historical research, to ...

Research cases work for any hypothesis-driven research. For hundreds of years, formal research has been published as static paper documents. The advent of the Internet has given us motivation to look at new approaches.

Preservation of the research process is key. It enables collaboration and profound, powerful publication. Any researcher should be able to publish. Research should be judged based on its quality. Sharing negative results will improve critical thinking.

Design Goals and Rationale

Research cases satisfies the following design goals:

Lightweight
Easy to share; flexible.
Simplicity
Easy for people to build with.
Domain agnostic
Should work for any domain of knowledge. Should also be able interoperate across domains of knowledge.

Terminology

General Terminology

This document uses the following terms:
Data
Data is raw, uninterpreted stuff.
Information
Information is interpreted data.
Evidence
Evidence is information interpreted in the context of the research question.
